Personal Goals

The whole hub is oriented around four beach volleyball outcomes: read the game better, jump higher, last longer, and hit the sand without donating skin to the court.

01

Solid defense: read, hold, dig, reset. Less guessing, more annoying your opponents.

02

Higher jumps: elastic ankles, stronger hips, cleaner approach rhythm.

03

Endurance: repeat rallies, sand legs, tournament-day energy management.

04

Diving without injury: controlled take-off, slide, shoulder position and recovery.

Weekly plan + taper

Weekly Plan

Built from the coaching recommendation: strength twice, skill quality, recovery protected.
MonStrength A + controlled games
  • Lunch: lower body, ankle and core strength.
  • Night: casual games at controlled intensity.
  • Keep it moderate-hard, not a leg funeral.
TueOptional light skills
  • Light pepper or ball control only if fresh.
  • No extra conditioning.
  • Rest if sleep, soreness or joints say no.
WedCoached / skill session
  • Passing, reading and defensive movement.
  • Serve receive reps and partner communication.
  • Stop before fatigue turns quality into confetti.
ThuStrength B power + prehab
  • Lunch: approach jumps, lateral bounds, upper body.
  • Shoulder, elbow and ankle prehab.
  • Night: rest or very light pepper only.
FriRecovery lock
  • Rest, mobility and hydration.
  • Protect sleep and fuel.
  • No heroic last-minute leg destruction.
SatMain volleyball load
  • Main drills and 2v2 work.
  • Casual 3v3 / 4v4 only if legs are still good.
  • Track hydration and carbs during long sessions.
SunTechnical + recovery-biased
  • Lower-intensity skills or self-organized drills.
  • Add defensive speed block if fresh.
  • Stop before the legs become noodles.

Load Rule

Cap hard days at three: Monday strength + games, Thursday strength/power and Saturday main volleyball.

Readiness

Before training, rate sleep, leg soreness, motivation, joint pain and energy. If three or more are bad, reduce intensity 30–50%.

Hydration

Long beach sessions start at 500–750ml fluid per hour, with electrolytes and carbs for sessions over 90 minutes.

Progress

Test approach jump every two weeks. Track soreness, ankle confidence, late-game defense, hydration and elbow soreness.

Tournament Taper

The goal is springy and sharp, not “accidentally peaked in training and now my calves have unionized.”

Wed / Thu

Keep it light-moderate: 20–30 min pepper, serve receive, defensive reads, partner communication and short 2v2 games. Avoid heavy legs, max jumps and endless diving reps.

Fri Primer

20–25 min only: easy mobility, bodyweight squats, reverse lunges, calf raises, 3×3 approach jumps at 70–80% and 5–8 easy serves.

Game Day Warm-Up

Jog or shuffle, mobilise hips/ankles/shoulders, then 2 rounds of pogos, lateral shuffles, building approach jumps, serve receive and controlled defensive moves.

Between Games

Sip fluids, eat small carbs, stay out of sun where possible and keep shoulders warm. Tiny choices, annoyingly large payoff.

Prep + mobility

Warmups

Warmups and maintenance pulled from the coaching recommendation: land quietly, protect ankles and shoulders, and avoid turning every session into a survival documentary.

Warmups & stretches

Training Warm-Up

Use before normal beach sessions.

  • 5 min easy jog, shuffle and sand movement.
  • Dynamic hips, ankles and shoulders.
  • 10 pogo hops + 6 lateral shuffles each way.
  • 5 approach jumps, gradually building.
  • 5 serve-receive passes and 5 controlled defensive moves into platform.

Friday Primer

Use the day before a tournament or hard match day.

  • 5 min easy mobility.
  • 2×10 bodyweight squats.
  • 2×8 reverse lunges each side.
  • 2×10 calf raises.
  • 3×3 approach jumps at 70–80% + 5–8 easy serves.

Strength A Warm-Up

Before lower-body strength.

  • Ankle circles: 10 each direction.
  • World’s greatest stretch: 5 each side.
  • Glute bridges: 15.
  • Bodyweight squats: 12.
  • Lateral lunges: 8 each side + pogo hops 2×20 sec.

Strength B Warm-Up

Before power, upper body and prehab.

  • Band pull-aparts: 15.
  • Scapular push-ups: 10.
  • Hip airplanes or single-leg balance: 5 each side.
  • Lateral shuffles: 2×10m.
  • Low pogo hops: 2×15 sec.

Ankle Routine

3× per week, 8 minutes, after training or at home.

  • Single-leg balance: 2×45 sec each side.
  • Single-leg balance with head turns: 2×30 sec each side.
  • Calf raises: 3×15.
  • Tibialis raises: 3×20.
  • Lateral line hops: 2×20 sec each side.

Shoulder / Elbow Prehab

3× per week. Boring, useful, annoyingly effective.

  • Band external rotation: 3×15.
  • Band pull-aparts: 3×15.
  • Scapular push-ups: 2×10.
  • Wrist extensor eccentrics: 3×12.
  • Forearm pronation / supination: 2×10 each side.

Controlled Sprawl Progression

For safer diving mechanics.

  • Low lunge reach.
  • Knee slide / hip drop.
  • Controlled sprawl.
  • Full dive only when clean.
  • Reach through ball, land on forearms/side body, then roll or slide.

Post-Session Downshift

Cool down before leaving the beach.

  • Calf stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Hip flexor stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Figure-four glute stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Lat and shoulder stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Slow nasal breathing: 5 breaths.

Squat Pattern

Use goblet squat or barbell squat. Stay tall, brace, lower with control and drive up cleanly.

  • Hard but clean. No ugly grinder reps.
  • Train power, not survival.

Romanian Deadlift

Hinge at hips, soft knees, long spine. Lower slowly until hamstrings load, then stand tall.

Bulgarian Split Squat

Back foot elevated, front foot stable. Build single-leg strength for takeoff, deceleration and recovery steps.

Hip Thrust / Glute Bridge

Drive through heels, squeeze glutes at top, keep ribs down. Do not turn it into a lower-back performance art piece.

Standing Calf Raise

Rise tall, pause at the top and lower slowly. Use support for balance when needed.

Tibialis Raise

Lean against a wall and lift toes toward shins. Useful for ankle durability and shin control.

Dead Bug

Low back stays gently connected to the floor while opposite arm and leg extend.

Side Plank

Elbow under shoulder, ribs stacked and hips lifted. Boringly controlled is the whole point.

Approach Jump

Fast last two steps, big arm swing, tall chest, jump up not forward, land quietly.

  • Rest 60–90 sec between crisp reps.
  • Test max jump every two weeks, not every session.

Lateral Bounds

Push sideways, land on one leg and stick for one second. Trains defender movement and knee/ankle control.

Push-Up

Hands under shoulders, ribs controlled, full-body tension. Elevate hands if needed.

Pull-Ups / Band Rows

Pull elbows toward ribs, keep shoulders away from ears. Use assistance or bands as needed.

Single-Leg RDL

Single-leg hinge with hips square. Reach long, then drive through standing foot.

Band External Rotation

Elbow near side, rotate forearm out without twisting torso. Keep it pain-free.

Band Face Pull

Pull band toward face with elbows high, shoulder blades moving smoothly.

Wrist Extensor Eccentrics

Use a light dumbbell. Help the weight up, then lower slowly with wrist extensors.

Pronation / Supination

Hold a light dumbbell or hammer-like object and rotate palm up/down under control.

Tempo Goblet Squat

Hold dumbbell or backpack. Use 3 seconds down, 1 second pause, then stand tall.

Single-Leg Hip Thrust

One foot planted, one leg lifted. Drive hips up without arching your lower back.

Pogo Hops

Small quick ankle hops with tall posture. Keep knees soft and contacts quiet.

Squat Jump

Dip under control, jump vertically and land softly. Stop when jump height drops.

Bar Hang / Scapular Pull

Hang from bar if available. Pull shoulder blades down slightly, then relax with control.

Split-Step Reaction Drill

Partner points left/right/short/deep. Stay low, first step explosive, platform early.

Short-Ball Pickup

Partner tosses short balls randomly. Sprint first, lower hips, set platform before contact.

Deep-Line / Deep-Angle Chase

Start middle-back. Partner calls line or angle. Open hips, turn and run, then platform.

Controlled Sprawl

Progress from low lunge reach to knee slide, hip drop, controlled sprawl and then full dive only when clean.

  • Keep chin up and reach through the ball.
  • Land on forearms/side body, not elbow point.

Defender Repeat Efforts

20 sec hard movement: shuffle, drop, recover and short sprint. Rest 40 sec. Repeat 10 rounds.

Ball-Based Conditioning

Partner feeds short, deep, angle and free-ball transition. Do 6 balls per rally simulation for 6–8 rounds.

Recovery Breathing

Slow nasal inhale, longer exhale. Use between rounds or after a hot session.

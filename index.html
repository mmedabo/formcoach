Personal Goals

The whole hub is oriented around four beach volleyball outcomes: read the game better, jump higher, last longer, and hit the sand without donating skin to the court.

01

Solid defense: read, hold, dig, reset. Less guessing, more annoying your opponents.

02

Higher jumps: elastic ankles, stronger hips, cleaner approach rhythm.

03

Endurance: repeat rallies, sand legs, tournament-day energy management.

04

Injury Prevention: controlled take-off, slide, shoulder position and recovery.

Tournament Taper

The point is to arrive springy and sharp, not “I peaked during Wednesday training and now my calves are filing a complaint.”

T-7 to T-5

Keep intensity, trim volume. One match-play session, one jump tune-up, no new max lifts.

T-4 to T-3

Prioritise serve receive, defensive reads, partner calls and short sharp rallies. Stop before quality drops.

T-2

Light sweat, mobility, a few controlled approaches. Hydrate early instead of trying to chug your way to excellence.

T-1 / Day

Walk-through, visualise first three points, pack gear, sleep. Warm up gradually and keep your first jumps submaximal.

Competition prep

Tournament Day

Keep the routine simple: get warm, stay fueled between games, and avoid turning the waiting time into a slow-motion sun roast.

Game day checklist

Warm-Up

  • 5 min light jog/shuffle.
  • Dynamic hips, ankles, shoulders.
  • 2 rounds: 10 pogo hops.
  • 6 lateral shuffles each way.
  • 5 approach jumps, gradually building.
  • 5 serve-receive passes.
  • 5 controlled defensive moves into platform.

Between Games

  • Sip fluids regularly.
  • Eat small carbs: banana, sports drink, bread, rice ball, energy bar.
  • Stay out of the sun where possible.
  • Keep shoulders warm, not stiff.

Skill-set filter

Defensive Speed Block

20–25 min quality block: split-step reactions 4×45 sec, short-ball pickups 4×6 reps, and deep-line/deep-angle chases 4×5 reps. Stay low, move first step fast, and platform early.

Controlled Sprawl Progression

3×5 each side: low lunge reach, knee slide or hip drop, controlled sprawl, then full dive only when clean. Reach through the ball and land on forearms/side body, not the elbow point.

Prep + mobility

Warmups

Warm up for sand: ankles, hips, shoulders, core temperature and controlled landing mechanics before the first rally tries to humble you.

Warmups & stretches

10-Min Sand Warm-Up

Use before normal training.

  • 2 min easy jog + side shuffle.
  • 1 min ankle hops and calf pulses.
  • 2 min hip openers, leg swings and lunges.
  • 2 min shoulder circles, scap push-ups and band pulls.
  • 3 min pepper build-up from easy to game speed.

Dive Prep

Keep volume low. Quality beats theatrical sand-eating.

  • Wrist circles and palm pressure rocks.
  • Kneeling slide pattern on soft sand.
  • Low sprawl to side, then stand and reset.
  • Reach, slide, recover footwork without chasing impossible balls.

Jump Prep

Prime elastic ankles and approach timing.

  • 20 pogo hops.
  • 10 squat-to-reach reps.
  • 6 approach jumps at 60–75%.
  • 3 full approach jumps only after legs feel springy.

Post-Session Stretch

Downshift before leaving the beach.

  • Calf stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Hip flexor stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Figure-four glute stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Lat and shoulder stretch: 45 sec per side.
  • Breathing: 5 slow nasal breaths.

Exercise reference library. Links from workouts open the matching card directly.

Ankle Circles

Move each ankle slowly through full circles. Keep pressure light and controlled.

World’s Greatest Stretch

Long lunge position with rotation toward the front leg. Use it to open hips, hamstrings and upper back.

Glute Bridge

Drive through heels, lift hips, pause at the top and keep ribs down.

Bodyweight Squat

Feet planted, knees track over toes, chest tall. Move smoothly rather than rushing reps.

Lateral Lunge

Step wide, sit into one hip, keep the other leg long. Push back to center with control.

Squat Pattern

Use goblet squat or barbell squat. Hard but clean reps only; no ugly grinder reps.

Romanian Deadlift

Hinge from the hips, keep the back long, lower under control and feel hamstrings load.

Hip Thrust

Upper back supported, feet planted, drive hips up and squeeze glutes without over-arching the back.

Tibialis Raise

Lean against a wall and lift toes toward shins. Lower slowly to build shin and ankle control.

Band Pull-Apart

Hold a light band, pull hands apart and keep shoulders down. Use smooth reps.

Lateral Bound

Jump sideways and stick the landing for one second. Keep knee and ankle stacked.

Push-Up

Hands under shoulders, body long, lower with control and press the floor away.

Pull-Up / Band Row

Choose pull-ups, assisted pull-ups or band rows. Pull shoulder blades back without shrugging.

Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

Hinge on one leg, keep hips mostly square and reach the free leg back for balance.

Band External Rotation

Elbow near ribs, rotate forearm outward against light band tension. Keep it pain-free.

Band Face Pull

Pull band toward face with elbows high, then control back to start. Avoid neck tension.

Wrist Extensor Eccentric

Assist the wrist up, then lower slowly against a light weight. Keep elbow and wrist calm.

Pronation / Supination Control

Hold a light dumbbell or hammer-like object and rotate forearm slowly each way.

Tempo Goblet Squat

Use a dumbbell or backpack. Lower for 3 seconds, pause 1 second, then stand tall.

Single-Leg Hip Thrust

One foot planted, one leg lifted, drive hips up evenly and pause briefly at the top.

Squat Jump

Dip quickly, jump straight up, land quietly and reset before the next rep.

Pull-Up Hang / Scapular Pull

Hang from a bar with active shoulders. For scapular pulls, move shoulder blades without bending elbows much.

Single-Leg Balance

Stand tall on one foot and keep tripod pressure through the foot. Use light support if needed.

Balance With Head Turns

Hold single-leg balance while slowly turning your head. Keep the ankle active, not panicked.

Lateral Line Hop

Hop side to side over a line with quiet contacts and steady knee tracking.

Pogo Jumps

Small quick ankle hops with tall posture. Keep knees soft and contacts quiet.

  • Use as a primer, not a fatigue contest.
  • Stop if Achilles or shin pain appears.

Approach Jump

Three-step or four-step approach into vertical jump. Land balanced and reset fully between reps.

Rear-Foot Elevated Split Squat

Back foot elevated, front foot stable. Lower under control, drive up through the whole foot.

Single-Leg Calf Raise

Rise tall, pause briefly, lower slowly. Use support for balance if needed.

Dead Bug

Low back stays gently connected to the floor while opposite arm and leg extend.

Tempo Sand Run

Comfortably hard pace on sand. You should breathe hard but still keep form.

Court Shuttle

Run between court lines with clean turns. Decelerate first, then push off.

Lateral Shuffle

Stay low, chest up, feet outside hips. Do not click heels together like a confused tap dancer.

Recovery Breathing

Slow nasal inhale, longer exhale. Use between rounds or after a hot session.

Scap Push-Up

Arms straight, shoulder blades move around the ribcage. Keep neck relaxed.

Side Plank

Elbow under shoulder, ribs stacked, hips lifted. Make it boringly controlled.

Bear Crawl

Hands under shoulders, knees hovering, move opposite hand and foot together.

Hip Airplane

Single-leg hinge with small hip rotation. Hold support until balance improves.

Controlled Dive Slide

Practice from low height on soft sand. Reach, slide, keep shoulder relaxed, then recover to feet.
